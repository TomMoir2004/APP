Generalised Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

What is GAD?

Generalised anxiety disorder (GAD) is a common mental health condition where you often feel very anxious about lots of different things.

This page is about adults aged 18 and over with GAD.

Symptoms

The main symptom of GAD is persistent worry or stress that disrupts your daily life. Other symptoms include:

  • Difficulty sleeping
  • Feeling restless or irritable
  • Fatigue
  • Stomach problems
  • Palpitations
  • Lightheadedness
  • Low mood or depression

If You're Not Sure It's GAD

You might have GAD if you feel anxious most of the time and it affects your daily life.

When to See a GP

  • If you think you have GAD
  • If your treatment is not helping

Other Help Options

You can refer yourself to an NHS talking therapies service without seeing a GP.

Support is also available for those pregnant or recently given birth.

Diagnosis

A GP will ask about your worries and symptoms. Be open and honest to get the right help.

Treatment

  • Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT)
  • Antidepressants like SSRIs
  • Referral to community mental health teams

Things You Can Try

Do:

  • Talk to someone
  • Use self-help resources
  • Learn calming techniques
  • Exercise and sleep well

Do Not:

  • Avoid problems
  • Use alcohol or drugs
  • Drink excessive caffeine

Support

Support is available from your GP, charities like Mind, Rethink Mental Illness, and Anxiety UK.

Causes

GAD may be more likely if you have:

  • A history of anxiety, trauma, or chronic illness
  • A family history of mental health conditions
  • Alcohol or drug misuse
