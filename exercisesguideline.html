Exercise Guidelines

NHS advice about healthy living, including eating a balanced diet, healthy weight, exercise, quitting smoking and drinking less alcohol.

Why Exercise is Important

Benefits of exercise:
Exercise can reduce your risk of major illnesses and lower your risk of early death by up to 30%. This includes the age groups:

  • Children (under 5 years)
  • Children and young people (5 to 18 years)
  • Adults (19 to 64 years)
  • Older adults (65 years and over)

Health benefits include reduced risk of:

  • Heart disease and stroke
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Cancer
  • Depression and dementia
  • Falls and fractures

What Counts?

Adults should aim for 150 minutes of activity per week — ideally daily.
Even light to moderate activity (like walking or cycling) counts, especially if it raises your heart rate and makes you breathe faster.

A Modern Problem

Technology and sedentary lifestyles have made inactivity a health risk.
Sitting for long periods slows metabolism and raises health risks even for active people.

Tips to Reduce Sitting Time

Under 5s:

  • Limit time in prams, buggies, or high chairs
  • Minimize screen time

Children (5–18):

  • Limit screen time
  • Encourage chores and physical gifts (e.g. scooters)

Adults (19–64):

  • Take the stairs
  • Walk while on phone calls
  • Break up long sitting sessions

Older Adults (65+):

  • Join walking or dance groups
  • Stay active with housework or play
  • Move during TV breaks

Move More, Sit Less

UK Chief Medical Officers recommend breaking up long sitting time with at least light activity. Even if you meet weekly exercise targets, extended sitting still poses risks.
